Chicken Brooding Using Biogas

In order to effectively utilize all the biogas produced, we have developed tools and solutions like the biogas brooder for both domestic and commercial brooding purposes.

Chicken Brooding System Overview

Commercial Use: Floor Heating System

  • As opposed to the traditional brooding using charcoal or expensive LPG gas, Biogas is a sustainable alternative for poultry brooding.
  • The system is powered by the chicken dropping, effectively managing the animal waste as well as produce valuable organic fertilizer.
  • Our innovative solution involves setting up a floor heating system in the room.
  • The system is comprised of heated water being channeled through a piped grid network that is placed on the floor of the brooding room.
  • This creates a cushion of warm air 33°- 35°, 8-12 inches high above the heated floor area.
  • The grid is activated in sections based on the number and age of the chicks, and can be easily expanded as the birds grow.
Brooding with Biogas - Commercial Installations

Efficient Floor Heating

Efficiency

  • Because the system relies on radiant heat, only the relevant floor area is heated and energy is not wasted heating the entire room, resulting in significant energy savings.
  • The absence of 'fire' in the brooding space eliminates pollutants, allowing for insulation and proper ventilation for fresh air.
  • This floor brooding system can also utilize other fuels such as recycled paper briquettes, agro waste briquettes, and solar heated water.
  • This floor heating method is an efficient and sustainable solution, utilizing 75% less energy than traditional air brooding systems, resulting in significant cost savings.

Domestic Brooding Setup

Domestic Use

  • An elevated enclosed brooder box containing the chicks traps the rising heat from a biogas fueled, or other pollutant free space heater beneath.
  • The temperature is simply controlled by adjusting the flame size or temperature of the heater.
  • Poop and spilled food that falls through the wire-mesh floor of the box is fed back into the digester.
  • The system is a closed cycle with gas out one end for heating the chicks and high quality fertilizer out the other.
